The Life of Harriet Tubman

"I was the conductor on the Underground Railroad for eight years, and I can say what most conductors can't say--
I never ran my train off the track and I never lost a passenger."

Portrait of young Harriet Tubman -- "baddass"

Timeline

1821 Born a slave

1844 Marries freeman, John Tubman

1849 Escapes from slavery leaving behind her parents, brother, sister and husband.

1851 First arrives in Canada

1854 Rescues three of her brothers.

1857 Rescues her parents and settles them in Auburn, NY

After escaping, Harriet returned 13 times over an 11 year period to lead and assist over 300 enslaved Africans to freedom.

1860 Begins addressing Women's Rights, Anti-Slavery and Negro Conventions

1861 Becomes involved in the war effort and by 1862, she joins the Department of the South at Beauford, S.C. as a "nurse, scout and spy."

1863 June 2nd, Harriet leads the Combahee Raid, freeing over 750 enslaved Africans.

1868Her first authorised biography was published.

1869 Marries Nelson Davis, 22 years younger. They remain married for 20 years before his death.

1888 Nelson Davis dies. In 1890, Harriet begins receiving a pension as a widow of a veteran.

1899 Begins receiving a veterans pension. It took 34 years for her to receive her own veterans pension

1896 She buys a 25 acre plot which was later turned over to the A.M.E. Zion Church and made into a home for the aged and indigent.
The Harriet Tubman home opens in 1908.

1913 Harriet dies on March 13th around age 91. She was buried with military honors.
